Introduction¶

In this project we'll be analyzing the No-show appointments dataset which can be found here. In particular, we'll be interested in finding trends among patients who showed up for their medical appointments, and how they differed from those patients who did not. The dataset contains the following columns which are described as follows:

  • PatientId = Identification of a patient
  • AppointmentID = Identification of each appointment
  • Gender = Male or Female
  • ScheduledDay = The day someone called or registered for the appointment
  • AppointmentDay = The day of the actual appointment
  • Age = How old is the patient
  • Neighbourhood = Where the appointment takes place
  • Scholarship = True of False
  • Hipertension = True or False
  • Diabetes = True or False
  • Alcoholism = True or False
  • Handcap = The number of disabilites a patient has
  • SMS_received = True or False
  • No-show = True or False

From these factors we will determine which have the most impact on a patient showing up for their scheduled appointment. Here are some relevant questions we'll be answering:

  1. Does age determine no show rates?
  2. Does receiving an SMS notification affect no show rates?
  3. Does the wait time between scheduling and the actual appointment determine no show rates?
  4. Does gender affect no show rates?
  5. Does having a disability affect no show rates?
  6. Does having a pre-existing condition such as hypertension, diabetes and/or alcoholism determine no show rates?

# define bar_plot function
def bar_plot(locations, heights, labels, colors, title, xlabel, ylabel, xoff, yoff):
    plt.figure(figsize=(12, 8))
    sns.set_context('talk')
    bars = plt.bar(locations, heights, tick_label=labels, color=colors)
    for bar in bars:
        yval = bar.get_height().round(1)
        plt.text(bar.get_x() + xoff, yval + yoff, yval)
    plt.title(title, pad=20)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel, labelpad=20)
    plt.ylabel(ylabel, labelpad=20);

Conclusions¶

Below is the summary of our findings. As a reminder the average no show rate for all patients is 20%.

Age - Older patients (56 and above) are least likely to miss their appointments, having only a 15% no show rate compared to the average which is 20%. Next are patients aged 38-55 with a 19% no show rate which is still better than average. Younger patients aged 0-18 are more likely to miss their appointments (22% vs the 20% average). And finally patients between 19-37 years old have the highest no show rate at 23%.

SMS Received - Patients who received SMS notifications were 5% less likely to miss their appointments than those who did not (27% vs 32% no show). Interestingly enough both figures are higher than the general average. Our analysis was limited by having to drop some samples so this may account for the disparity.

Wait Time - There is a direct correlation between the waiting period and the rate of no shows. As the wait time increases, so does the likelihood of missed appointments. Same date appointments have the lowest no show rate at 4%. From there up to a week the no show rate increases to 24%, after 2 weeks 33% of patients miss their appointments.

Gender - Our analysis shows this variable had practically no impact on the rate of missed appointments.

Disability - Persons with disabilities are 2% less likely to miss their appointments compared to the average.

Pre-existing condition - Patients with hypertension or diabetes are less likely to miss their appointments compared to the average by a difference of 3% and 2% respectively. Meanwhile alcoholism doesn't seem to play a factor in the no show rate. But keep in mind we did not factor in patients with 2 or more conditions so this limits our analysis.

Out of all the variables we explored, the most impactful were Wait time, Age and SMS Received, in that order. Those 3 factors are what we should consider when predicting if a patient makes their scheduled appointment. To be clear, there were limitations to our analysis. We did not include scholarship and neighborhood in our observations. Nor did we use more advanced statistical methods as that is beyond the scope of this project. That being said, we can end the report with a fair amount of confidence in our methodology and in the accuracy of our findings.
